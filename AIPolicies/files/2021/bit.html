BIT - Standards AI Guidance

State of South Dakota Generative Artificial Intelligence (AI) Guidelines & Acceptable State Use

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
Generative Artificial Intelligence, or Generative AI, refers to a category of artificial intelligence systems and techniques that are designed to generate content similar to what a human might produce. These systems use machine learning algorithms to create new data or content, often based on patterns and examples in the data from which they have been trained. The technology industry is changing rapidly, and this technology is not accurate 100% of the time. Because of this many organizations have banned Generative AI use, while others allow unrestricted usage. The State recognizes the opportunity for a controlled and responsible approach that acknowledges the benefits to efficiency while minimizing the risks around AI bias, privacy, and cybersecurity.

BIT recognizes the potential benefits of using artificial intelligence (AI) to improve efficiency and productivity in the workplace for state government. However, we also acknowledge the importance of using AI responsibly and ethically, particularly when it comes to generating content.

These guidelines for state government agencies aim to provide detail for the responsible use of AI-generated content, emphasizing the need for proofing, editing, fact-checking, and using AI-generated content as a starting point, not the finished product.

These guidelines will be replaced in the future with policies and standards. BIT will include these technologies in future tech topic webinars for those interested in learning more about these technologies.

APPLICATION OF THE GUIDELINES
The use of these guidelines applies to all executive branch state employees, contractors, volunteers or others using state equipment and networks.

Agencies may introduce additional rules on the usage of Generative AI for their staff, as long as these guidelines are the minimum standard.

GENERAL GUIDELINES FOR AI USE ON STATE DEVICES

  1. Public information only: All information entered into a Generative AI system may be viewed and available to others. Consider all information entered as public and do not enter sensitive or confidential information.
  2. Authorized Use: Employees are authorized to use AI for work-related purposes only. This includes tasks such as research, data analysis, and communication with clients and colleagues.
  3. Personal Use: Use of Generative AI systems for personal reasons falls within the scope of the state acceptable use policy.
  4. Prohibited Activities: Employees must not use AI systems for any activities that may be illegal or unethical, including but not limited to spreading false information, engaging in cyberbullying or harassment, or attempting to gain unauthorized access to any systems or networks.

REQUIREMENTS FOR USER REQUESTED AI-GENERATED CONTENT

  1. Proofing: All AI-generated content must be proofread and checked for accuracy by a human before being published or shared. This includes checking for spelling errors, grammar mistakes, and factual inaccuracies.
  2. Editing: AI-generated content must be edited to ensure that it is well-written, coherent, and engaging. This includes ensuring that the content is structured in a logical manner and that it is appropriate for the intended audience.
  3. Fact-checking: AI-generated content must be fact-checked to ensure that all information is accurate and up to date. This includes verifying sources, checking statistics, and ensuring that any claims made in the content are supported by evidence.
  4. Accountability and responsibility: If you use AI systems you, as the user is responsible for any content published and used.
  5. Starting point, not the finished product: AI-generated content should be viewed as a starting point, not the finished product. While AI can provide a valuable tool for generating content, it cannot replace the creativity and critical thinking skills of human writers and editors.
  6. Transparency: All AI-generated content must be clearly labeled as such, and the use of AI in generating content should be transparent to users. Users must also note the AI model being used in the reference.
  7. Data privacy: We must ensure that any personal or sensitive data used to train AI models is handled with appropriate care and that any AI-generated content that contains such data is handled in compliance with data protection laws.

CAUTIONS

  1. Do not share proprietary, sensitive, or confidential data with AI systems.
    1. As an AI language model, AI Systems are designed to process and generate language based on the input it receives. While AI Systems are sophisticated tools that can provide helpful insights and responses, it is not inherently equipped to handle sensitive information.
    2. Since AI systems are digital tools that can potentially be accessed by others, it may not be appropriate to share proprietary and confidential information with it. There is a risk that the information could be exposed or misused, either through a security breach or by unintended parties gaining access.
  2. AI Hallucinations
    1. Artificial Intelligence hallucinations are when an AI generates unexpected, untrue results that are not backed by real-world data. AI hallucinations can be false content, news, or information about people, events, or facts. They can also be deviations from external facts, contextual logic, or both.
  3. Compliance with Federal data protection laws
    1. Unless specifically granted, and documented by federal agencies, no data that is governed by federal law may be used with an AI system.
  4. Bias and discrimination
    1. AI systems can reflect and amplify human biases and prejudices. Users of AI system must ensure that use does not discriminate against individuals or groups based on protected characteristics such as race, gender, or age.

REPORTING INCIDENTS
For any concerns related to the use of Generative AI, or if you become aware of any incidents using sensitive, confidential or privacy restricted information, report the incident to the BIT service Desk.

EXAMPLE USE CASE AND PROMPT EXAMPLES

  1. Writing position specific interview questions. Generative AI can be used to create lists of potential questions aligned to specific job descriptions and similar contextual based generation of questions.
    1. Example Prompt Steps:
      1. Copy the public facing job description from the BHR job posting.
      2. Login to a generative AI and provide the following prompt:
      3. As the hiring manager, write a set of interview questions for the below job description. Include questions that focus on individual leadership and attention to detail. (Then paste the job description below)
      4. Follow-up clarifying prompts can be entered such as, ‘limit to 10 questions that address the key functions of the position’ or ‘re-write the questions in the style of Dr. Suess’
  2. Creating a training class outline. Generative AI can create program outlines, policy drafts, memos and similar documents.
    1. Example prompt steps:
      1. Define a high-level class goal, subject etc. For this example, we will use ‘prompt engineering’.
      2. Login to a generative AI and provide the following prompt:
      3. Create a training module for employees on prompt engineering. Limit the training to 2 hours and cover core topics of AI prompt engineering. Pre-requisites include completion of Intro to Generative AI.
  3. Create a set of best practices for [operation]. Ask Generative AI to create lists as if they were experts in any field.
    1. Example prompt steps:
      1. Login to a generative AI and provide the following prompt:
      2. Create a list of best practices for application of GASB 96. Include steps for classification of the SPITA.
      3. Alternatives to this could be "write a policy for the adherence to GASB 96 for all state procurement teams. Include details on how to classify SPITA purchases”
